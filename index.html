About Microgreens


Microgreens are young, edible plants harvested just after the first true leaves have developed.

They are miniature versions of vegetables and herbs, picked at an early growth stage.

They’re known for their intense flavors and powerful nutritional punch, often containing higher concentrations of vitamins, minerals and antioxidants than their mature counterparts.

It is typically harvested within 7 to 21 days after germination.

They’re typically small, packed with flavor, and loaded with nutrients, making them a popular choice for salads, sandwiches, and garnishes.



Benefits of Microgreens



Microgreens offer a variety of benefits, including:

  • Nutrient Density

    Microgreens are packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. They often contain higher concentrations of nutrients than their mature counterparts.


  • Flavor Enhancement

    They add a burst of flavor to dishes, ranging from peppery to sweet, enhancing the overall taste of salads, sandwiches, and more.


  • Versatility

    Microgreens can be used in various culinary applications, from garnishes to main ingredients, making them a versatile addition to any meal.


  • Digestive Health

    Rich in fiber and beneficial compounds, microgreens can aid digestion and support gut health.


  • Antioxidant Properties

    Many varieties are high in antioxidants, which can help combat oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.


  • Visual Appeal

    Their vibrant colors and delicate textures make dishes more visually appealing, enhancing the overall dining experience.


  • Low-Calorie Addition

    Microgreens are low in calories, making them a great way to add flavor and nutrition without significantly increasing caloric intake.
